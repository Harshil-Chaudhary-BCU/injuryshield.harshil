Squats

Description - The squat targets the lower body muscles, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and lower back.
Benefits - Builds leg strength, enhances core stability, improves overall athleticism.
Equipments - Barbell, squat rack or power cage.

- How to perform a Squat

Start with feet shoulder-width apart, barbell resting on your upper back. Descend by bending your hips and knees, keeping your back straight. Lower until thighs are parallel to the ground. Push through heels to return to the starting position.

Beginner

Warm Up - Perform dynamic stretches and activation exercises with light to moderate intensity for 5-10 minutes.
Main Technique - Start with lighter weights, focusing on proper form and technique. Aim for 3-4 sets of 8-10 repetitions.
Cool Down - Perform static stretches and foam rolling exercises with gentle intensity for 5-10 minutes.

Advance

Warm Up - Incorporate more dynamic movements and heavier activation exercises with moderate to high intensity for 10-15 minutes.
Main Technique - Increase the weight gradually, focusing on lifting heavier loads with proper form. Aim for 4-6 sets of 4-6 repetitions with challenging weights.
Cool Down - Use deeper stretches and more intense foam rolling techniques with moderate intensity for 10-15 minutes.

Dead Lifts

Description - The deadlift targets the back, hips, glutes, hamstrings, and grip strength.
Benefits - Builds total body strength, enhances functional movement patterns.
Equipments - Barbell, weight plates.

- How to perform a Dead Lift

Stand with feet hip-width apart, grip the barbell with hands shoulder-width apart. Keep your back flat, hinge at the hips, and bend your knees slightly. Lift the barbell by driving through your heels and straightening your hips and knees.

Beginner

Warm Up - Start with hip mobility drills and light deadlifts with low to moderate intensity for 5-10 minutes.
Main Technique - Begin with lighter weights, focusing on proper form and technique. Aim for 3-4 sets of 8-10 repetitions.
Cool Down - Perform gentle lower back stretches and hamstring stretches with low intensity for 5-10 minutes.

Advance

Warm Up - Incorporate more dynamic hip mobility exercises and heavier deadlift warm-ups with moderate to high intensity for 10-15 minutes.
Main Technique - Progress to heavier weights, focusing on maximal strength and power. Aim for 4-6 sets of 4-6 repetitions with challenging weights.
Cool Down - Use deeper stretches and more intense foam rolling techniques with moderate intensity for 10-15 minutes.

Bench Press

Description - The bench press targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps.
Benefits - Increases upper body strength, improves pushing power.
Equipments - Barbell, bench press bench or flat bench.

- How to perform a Bench Press

Lie on a flat bench, grip the barbell slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower the barbell to your chest, then press it upwards until arms are fully extended.

Beginner

Warm Up - Start with shoulder mobility exercises and light push-up variations with low to moderate intensity for 5-10 minutes.
Main Technique - Begin with lighter weights, focusing on proper technique and control. Aim for 3-4 sets of 8-10 repetitions.
Cool Down - Perform gentle chest stretches and triceps stretches with low intensity for 5-10 minutes.

Advance

Warm Up - Incorporate more challenging shoulder mobility drills and heavier push-up variations with moderate to high intensity for 10-15 minutes.
Main Technique - Progress to heavier weights, focusing on explosive power and maximal strength. Aim for 4-6 sets of 4-6 repetitions with challenging weights.
Cool Down - Use deeper stretches and more intense stretches with moderate intensity for 10-15 minutes.
